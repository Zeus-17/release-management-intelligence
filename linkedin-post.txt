I've been building something.

Not because I needed another tool on my CV — but because I wanted to prove something about what's now possible when service delivery experience meets AI-assisted development.

The result: Release Management Intelligence — an open-source operational tool for Release Managers, Service Managers, Technical POs and Delivery Leads.

Here's what it does that I've never seen in a single place:

🎯 Release Readiness Score — a 0–100 score per release, live on the dashboard. Gates complete, blockers resolved, environments healthy, sign-offs done. No more "how are we looking?" — the number tells you.

🚦 Go/No-Go Decision Wizard — a 5-step structured modal that makes the most important moment in any release feel formal, documented and auditable. Who decided. When. Why. Timestamped.

🟢 Hypercare Mode — post go-live, a countdown clock, monitoring checklist and rollback criteria visible across every tab. Makes the scariest 72 hours feel managed.

📅 Blackout Calendar — change freezes, financial reporting periods, bank holidays, offshore team unavailability, system maintenance windows. All in one view. UK 2026–27 holidays pre-loaded.

🧵 Golden Thread — trace every scope item from Jira Align portfolio epic through to production, with references across 12 tools (Jira, ADO, ServiceNow, GitHub, Linear and more).

⬡ Deterministic AI — 17 pattern rules fire as you type. Payment keywords? Regulatory flag. Authentication changes? Rollback guidance. Database migration? DBA sign-off reminder. No external API. No data leaves the browser.

And it's all in a single HTML file. Open it. Works.

ITIL 4 aligned. DORA metrics tracked. EU DORA Article 9 aware. WCAG AA accessible. Dark/light/high contrast. Mobile responsive. Local-first. Open source.

This is what I mean when I say I'm actively upskilling — not just taking courses, but building real things with AI that solve real operational problems.
